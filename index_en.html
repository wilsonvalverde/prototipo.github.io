The Depression

Depression is a mood disorder that involves feeling persistent sadness and loss of interest. It is different from the mood fluctuations that people routinely experience as part of life. important life events, such as bereavement or job loss, can lead to depression. However, doctors only they consider that, if they are persistent, the feelings of pain are part of depression. The Depression it is an ongoing problem, not a temporary one. It consists of episodes during which symptoms last at least 2 weeks. Depression can last for several weeks, months, or years. (Goldman, L., 2020)

The Stress

Stress is considered to be the mechanism that is set in motion when a person is involved in an excess of situations that exceed their resources. Therefore, they are outnumbered try to meet the demands placed on you to overcome them. In these cases, the individual experience an overload that can influence physical, psychological and personal well-being. (Ponce, I.G., 2021)

Tips


Exercise is one of the most important things you can do to combat stress.

Decrease caffeine is a stimulant found in coffee, tea, chocolate and beverages energizing. Since in high doses they can increase anxiety.

Keeping a journal can help relieve stress and anxiety, especially if you focus on what positive.

Having strong social ties can help you cope with stressful times, reduce your risk of anxiety and depression.

Mindfulness practices can help reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression.

Maintain a good diet. depression can affect appetite.

When a person is depressed, they may have blocked creativity and the ability to enjoy of things, difficulties to express themselves. Exercise your imagination (painting, drawing, making doodling, sewing, writing, dancing, composing music, etc.)
